Privacy Policy

How we collect, use, and protect your personal data — in plain English.

Da Gym — Stratford, London E15
Data Controller: Da Gym  |  Contact: deanburt1308@gmail.com  |  Last updated: 28 June 2026

1. Introduction

Da Gym ("we", "us", "our") is committed to protecting your personal information. This Privacy Policy explains what personal data we collect about you, how we use it, who we share it with, and your rights under the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR) and the Data Protection Act 2018.

Please read this policy carefully. If you have any questions, contact us at deanburt1308@gmail.com or write to us at the address above.

2. What Personal Data We Collect

We collect and process the following categories of personal data:

2.1 Information you provide directly

  • Identity data: Full name, date of birth, gender.
  • Contact data: Home address, email address, telephone number.
  • Financial data: Bank account details for Direct Debit payments. We do not store full card numbers — payment processing is handled by our third-party payment provider.
  • Health data: Medical information or conditions you disclose to us (for example, when completing a PAR-Q health questionnaire or induction form). This is special category data and is treated with additional care.
  • Membership data: Membership plan, start date, visit history, class bookings, and personal training records.
  • Communications: Any messages, emails, or enquiries you send us.

2.2 Information we collect automatically

  • Entry data: Date, time, and duration of each gym visit recorded via our entry system.
  • CCTV footage: Images captured by our CCTV system for security purposes.
  • Website data: If you visit our website, we may collect your IP address, browser type, pages visited, and the date/time of your visit via cookies and analytics tools. See Section 8 (Cookies) for more detail.

3. How We Use Your Personal Data

We use your personal data for the following purposes and legal bases:

3.1 To perform our contract with you

  • Processing your membership application and managing your account.
  • Collecting membership fees and administering your Direct Debit.
  • Providing access to our facilities and services.
  • Booking personal training sessions and group classes.
  • Sending membership confirmation, receipts, and booking confirmations.

3.2 Legal obligation

  • Maintaining records for accounting, tax, and regulatory purposes.
  • Responding to requests from law enforcement or regulatory bodies where legally required.
  • Compliance with health and safety legislation.

3.3 Legitimate interests

  • Operating and improving our gym and services.
  • Monitoring gym usage to manage capacity and equipment.
  • CCTV monitoring to maintain safety and security of members and staff.
  • Investigating and responding to complaints or incidents.
  • Sending you service-related communications (e.g. changes to opening hours).
  • Fraud prevention and detection.

3.4 With your consent

  • Sending you marketing communications about our offers, events, new classes, and membership promotions — by email, SMS, or post. You can opt out at any time (see Section 7).
  • Sharing your before/after photos or testimonials on our website or social media (only with your explicit written consent).
  • Processing special category data (health information) where you have provided explicit consent for us to do so.

4. Who We Share Your Data With

We do not sell your personal data to any third party. We may share your data with:

  • Payment processors: To manage Direct Debit and card payments securely. These providers are bound by their own data protection obligations.
  • Gym management software providers: To manage membership records, bookings, and access control.
  • Email marketing platforms: If you have consented to receive marketing emails, your email address may be processed by our email service provider.
  • Accountants and legal advisers: Where necessary to manage our business, under strict confidentiality.
  • Law enforcement and regulatory authorities: Where we are legally required to do so, for example in response to a court order or police request.
  • Emergency services: In the event of a medical emergency or serious incident at our premises.

All third parties are required to process your data securely and in accordance with applicable data protection law.

5. How Long We Keep Your Data

We retain your personal data for as long as necessary to fulfil the purposes outlined in this policy, subject to the following:

  • Active membership records: Retained for the duration of your membership.
  • Post-membership records: Retained for up to 6 years after your membership ends for accounting, legal, and dispute resolution purposes.
  • Health / PAR-Q data: Retained for the duration of your membership and deleted within 12 months of your membership ending, unless there is an ongoing legal matter.
  • CCTV footage: Retained for up to 30 days and then automatically overwritten, unless required for an investigation or legal matter.
  • Marketing consent: Retained until you withdraw your consent or we determine you are no longer an active contact.
  • Enquiry / contact form data: Retained for up to 12 months.

6. Your Rights

Under UK GDPR, you have the following rights regarding your personal data:

Right of Access

You can request a copy of the personal data we hold about you (a "Subject Access Request").

Right to Rectification

You can ask us to correct inaccurate or incomplete personal data we hold about you.

Right to Erasure

You can ask us to delete your personal data in certain circumstances ("right to be forgotten").

Right to Restrict Processing

You can ask us to pause the processing of your data in certain circumstances.

Right to Data Portability

You can request your data in a structured, machine-readable format to transfer to another provider.

Right to Object

You can object to us processing your data on the basis of legitimate interests or for direct marketing.

Right to Withdraw Consent

Where processing is based on your consent, you can withdraw it at any time without affecting prior processing.

Right to Complain

You have the right to lodge a complaint with the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) at ico.org.uk.

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us at deanburt1308@gmail.com. We will respond within 30 days. We may need to verify your identity before fulfilling a request.

7. Marketing Communications

With your consent, we may contact you by email, SMS, or post with information about membership offers, new classes, events, and promotions.

You can opt out of marketing communications at any time by:

  • Clicking the "unsubscribe" link in any marketing email;
  • Emailing us at deanburt1308@gmail.com with the subject line "Unsubscribe";
  • Speaking to a member of staff at reception.

Opting out of marketing will not affect service communications (such as booking confirmations, receipts, or important membership updates).

8. Cookies

Our website uses cookies — small text files stored on your device — to improve your browsing experience. We use the following types of cookies:

  • Strictly necessary cookies: Required for the website to function. These cannot be disabled.
  • Analytics cookies: Help us understand how visitors use our website (e.g. Google Analytics). These are only set with your consent.
  • Marketing cookies: Used to show you relevant adverts on other platforms (e.g. Facebook Pixel). These are only set with your consent.

You can manage or disable non-essential cookies through your browser settings or by using our cookie consent tool when you first visit the site. Note that disabling cookies may affect the functionality of the website.

9. Data Security

We implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect your personal data against unauthorised access, loss, destruction, or alteration. These include:

  • Password-protected systems and encrypted storage for digital records.
  • Restricted access — only staff who need to access your data to do their job can do so.
  • Staff training on data protection and information security.
  • Secure disposal of paper records containing personal data.

In the event of a data breach that is likely to affect your rights and freedoms, we will notify you and the ICO in accordance with our legal obligations.

10. CCTV

We operate CCTV cameras throughout our premises (excluding changing rooms and toilets) for the purpose of maintaining the safety and security of members, staff, and property. CCTV footage is stored securely and retained for up to 30 days. Access to CCTV footage is restricted to authorised personnel. Footage may be shared with the police or other authorities if required by law or in connection with an investigation.

11. Children's Privacy

Our services are not directed at children under the age of 16. We do not knowingly collect personal data from children under 16 without verifiable parental consent. If you believe a child has provided us with personal data without appropriate consent, please contact us immediately.

12. Third-Party Websites

Our website may contain links to third-party websites (for example, our social media profiles). We are not responsible for the privacy practices of those websites and encourage you to read their privacy policies.

13. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices or legal requirements. We will post the updated policy on our website with a revised "last updated" date. Where changes are significant, we will notify you by email or by a notice on our website.
